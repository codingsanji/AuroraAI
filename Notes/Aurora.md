## Understanding The Aurora Phenomenons

``` Aurora is the latin word for "dawn" and is the name of the Roman goddess of the dawn. ```

**What is it?**
```Also known as the polar lights, the northern and southern lights are also known as the **Aurora Borealis** and **Aurora Australis** respectively.```

**When and how does it occur?**
```In short, they occur when high energy particles from the Sun collide with the neutral atoms in our atmosphere. The energy emitted from this crash/collide produces a spectacle of light that we now speak about. ```

Let's extrapolate on this by splitting the process into three steps:
**1. Leaving the Sun:**
``` 
The protons (+1 electric charge) and electrons (-1 electric charge) creating the northern lights depart from the Sun's corona (one of the hottest regions of the sun).

The intense heat of the corona causes the Sun's hydrogen and helium atoms to vibrate and shake off protons and electrons (as if stripping off layers on a hot day). Impatiently, these fast moving protons and electrons move too fast to be contained by the Sun's gravity and end up grouping together as a plasma, an electrically charged gas. 

```
